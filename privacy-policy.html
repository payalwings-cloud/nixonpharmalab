Privacy Policy

You agree to the terms of this privacy policy by using this website. You will be deemed to have granted Nixon Pharmalab Industries Ltd. (Nixon Pharmalab) permission to process your information as described in this policy if you submit your information to us.

Nixon Pharmalab respects each visitor's right to privacy and pledges to take all reasonable steps to safeguard visitors' personal information as well as their "sensitive personal data or information" (SPDI). Nixon Pharmalab also complies with all legal, regulatory, and/or contractual requirements pertaining to data privacy.

This policy addresses the handling, archiving, and retrieval of information as needed for legitimate, contractual, or other business-related operations with Nixon Pharmalab, as well as any other requirements. It outlines Nixon Pharmalab's policies and practices for gathering, using, and disclosing data submitted or received by real people.

Personal data that Nixon Pharmalab gathers, receives, stores, transmits, or processes, including: Passwords; Financial data like bank account, credit card, debit card, or other forms of financial instrument details; state of physical, physiological, and mental well-being; sexual orientation; past and present medical records; Biometric data; Any information pertaining to the aforementioned categories of personal data that customers have given Nixon Pharmalab in exchange for services; and Any personal data that Nixon Pharmalab has received and is processing, storing, or processing in accordance with a valid contract or otherwise.

A visitor's personal data is any information about them or a combination of details that would fairly allow them to be identified. Complete name, cell phone number, home address, email address, gender, and birthdate are examples of personal data.

In accordance with standard business procedures, Nixon Pharmalab will only gather the essential amount of data necessary to facilitate safe online identity verification, communication, and transactions with real people. The setting of cookies and the gathering of additional session data may fall under this category.

The collection and processing of personal data via our websites are conducted with utmost regard for privacy and in strict adherence to the prevailing legal standards. This meticulous attention to data governance is aimed at ensuring both compliance and the safeguarding of individual rights. The reasons for, or the legal bases upon which the personal data are processed, encompass the following:

1. Compliance with Legal Obligations: The processing of personal data may be necessary to fulfill legal requirements to which our organization is subject. This encompasses adhering to regulatory mandates, tax laws, and other statutory obligations under applicable jurisdiction. Our commitment to legal compliance ensures that all personal data handling is in strict observance of relevant laws and regulations.
2. Contractual Necessities: The execution or initiation of a contract with the data subject necessitates certain operations involving personal data. In these instances, data processing is pivotal to service delivery, product provision, or the performance of any other contractually agreed-upon tasks. This ensures that both parties can effectively fulfill their contractual obligations and duties.
3. Legitimate Interests Pursued by Our Organization or by a Third Party: There may be scenarios where the processing of personal data is vital for the purposes of the legitimate interests pursued by our organization or by a third party. This includes but is not limited to, situations involving direct marketing, fraud prevention, IT security, and within the scope of corporate restructuring or mergers and acquisitions. It is essential to note that such processing under legitimate interest does not unjustifiably infringe upon the rights and freedoms of the data subjects.
4. Consent: At times, the processing of personal data is carried out based on the explicit consent of the individual concerned. This consent is obtained in full compliance with legal standards, ensuring that individuals are fully informed and that their agreement is given freely, specific to the context, and unambiguous. This form of data processing allows individuals greater control over their personal information, contributing to transparency and trustworthiness in our operations.
5. Protection of Vital Interests: In exceptional cases, the processing of personal data may become imperative to protect the vital interests of the data subject or of another natural person. This could pertain to emergency situations where processing personal data is crucial for life-saving purposes or where significant harm could otherwise result.
6. Public Interest or Official Authority: Certain data processing activities might be necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority vested in the data controller. This includes activities that support public services, law enforcement efforts, and other duties performed for the benefit of the community at large.
Our organization is steadfast in its commitment to adhering to the principles of data protection and privacy. We prioritize the establishment of clear, lawful bases for any processing activity undertaken, ensuring that the rights and interests of data subjects are preserved and respected throughout.
